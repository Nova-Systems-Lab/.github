# Nova Systems Lab Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Nova Systems Lab is committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and constructive environment for everyone.

We welcome contributors and community members regardless of experience level, background, identity, education, nationality, or technical ability.

Participation in Nova Systems Lab spaces requires respectful and professional behaviour.

## Expected Behaviour

Community members are expected to:

- Treat others with respect and patience
- Give constructive and useful feedback
- Welcome beginners and help them learn
- Respect differing technical opinions
- Keep discussions relevant and productive
- Credit the work of others
- Protect private and sensitive information
- Accept moderation and maintainer decisions respectfully
- Focus criticism on ideas and code, not individuals

## Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviour is not acceptable:

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks
- Discrimination or hateful language
- Insults, humiliation, or deliberate hostility
- Sexual harassment or unwanted sexual attention
- Trolling, repeated disruption, or bad-faith arguments
- Publishing another person's private information
- Impersonating maintainers or contributors
- Spreading knowingly false project information
- Spam, scams, or unrelated advertising
- Sharing malware, stolen code, leaked files, piracy tools, or illegal content
- Pressuring contributors for features or release dates
- Claiming another person's work as your own
- Presenting unofficial builds as official Nova Systems Lab releases

## Technical Discussions

Constructive disagreement is welcome.

When reviewing ideas, code, documentation, or designs:

- Explain concerns clearly
- Provide evidence where possible
- Suggest alternatives
- Avoid dismissive or insulting language
- Distinguish confirmed facts from speculation
- Remember that contributors may be learning

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Nova Systems Lab community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories
- GitHub Issues
- Pull Requests
- GitHub Discussions
- Discord channels
- Project documentation
- Community events
- Other official communication spaces

It also applies when someone is officially representing Nova Systems Lab in public.

## Reporting Problems

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour:

1. Do not continue a hostile public argument.
2. Save relevant links, screenshots, or message references.
3. Contact the Nova Systems Lab moderation team privately at:

novasystemslab@gmail.com

4. Provide a clear explanation of what occurred.

Do not make public accusations containing private or sensitive information.

Reports will be reviewed as fairly and confidentially as reasonably possible.

## Enforcement

Maintainers and moderators may take actions including:

- Giving a private or public warning
- Removing content
- Locking conversations
- Restricting participation
- Temporarily muting or suspending a member
- Removing a contributor from a project
- Banning a member from community spaces
- Reporting serious abuse to the relevant platform

The response will depend on the severity, frequency, context, and impact of the behaviour.

## Good-Faith Moderation

Moderation decisions will aim to protect community safety, project integrity, and constructive participation.

Retaliation against someone for making a good-faith report is not permitted.

Deliberately false or malicious reports may themselves be treated as a violation.

## Questions

Questions about this Code of Conduct may be raised privately with the Nova Systems Lab organization owner or moderation team.
